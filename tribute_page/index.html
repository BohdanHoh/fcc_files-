Colonel Robert Green Ingersoll

The Great Agnostic

SUMMARY

Although practically unknown in modern society, Robert Green Ingersoll was one of the most prolific orators of 19th century America.

While he was practitioner of law and a politician, he was probably foremost known as an uncompromising champion of free thought during the age of enlightenment.

Significant events in the life of Robert G. Ingersoll

  • 1833 - Born in Dresden, New York to an abolitionist Presbyterian minister father.
  • 1854 - Admitted to the bar in Illinois after apprenticing under two lawyers.
  • 1861 - Raised the 11th Regiment Illinois Volunteer Calvary for the Union Army playing a roll at the battle of Shiloh.
  • 1862 - Married Eva Amelia Parker on Valentines Day.
  • 1867 - Appointed as the first Attorney General of Illinois.
  • 1899 - Died of congestive heart failure in Dobbs Ferry, NY.
